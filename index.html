About Me

My journey began with a search for true passion. For a long time, I explored subjects that I thought would satisfy my hunger for a meaningful career through academics, but they felt like plain books in disguise. I pursued law but soon realised the content didn’t resonate with me. I worked full time in hospitality, believing that climbing the ladder in a restaurant environment would meet my needs, but I still felt unfulfilled.

Later, I became an apprentice baker, hoping to plant the seeds of my problem-solving skills, and then moved into warehouse work to escape the rushed environment. Yet none of these paths truly quenched my thirst for something that intrigued me and allowed me to contribute to humanity.

That changed when I discovered The Odin Project, a self-taught online curriculum. In my free time, I had been curious about coding, and this opened a fresh perspective on my passion. Now I get to solve “puzzles” through projects and learn languages I genuinely want to learn for the sake of creating and solving problems — work that makes me feel alive. Although self-teaching can be challenging at times, I’m eager to learn from others, develop my skills further, and turn this passion into a career.
